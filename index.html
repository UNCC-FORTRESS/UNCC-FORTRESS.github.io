FORTRESS: Formal Techniques for Resilient and Secure Systems

We study formal methods for correctness and security of computer systems, with a focus on cyber physical systems. We develop reasoning techniques and enforcement mechanisms that support secure and dependable systems.

About

FORTRESS is a research group at UNC Charlotte working on formal foundations and tools for secure and reliable systems. Our work emphasizes mathematical proofs and verification techniques to show that no attack strategy can cause a system to misbehave.

Principal Investigator: Jian Xiang, Assistant Professor, Software and Information Systems, College of Computing and Informatics.

Research themes

  • Formal methods for security and resilience in cyber physical systems
  • Quantitative robustness analysis under sensor and timed attacks
  • Relational reasoning in dynamic logics
  • Information flow analysis and language based security
  • Runtime enforcement mechanisms grounded in formal guarantees
